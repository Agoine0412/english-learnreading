艺术市场的衰退2010 考研英语（二）· Text 1

The longest bull run in a century of art-market history ended on a dramatic note

难度 考研级 词汇·语法·结构 主题 · 艺术市场 长难句 7
Paragraph 1 — 艺术市场百年最长牛市以戏剧性方式终结
1
The longest bull run in a century of art-market history ended on a dramatic note with a sale of 56 works by Damien Hirst, Beautiful Inside My Head Forever, at Sotheby's in London on September 15th, 2008.
2008 年 9 月 15 日，伦敦苏富比拍卖行以达明安·赫斯特 56 件作品的拍卖《我脑海中永恒的美》戏剧性地结束了艺术市场史上持续百年的最长牛市。
🔍 长难句拆解
主句结构：
  主语 = The longest bull run（最长的牛市）
    后置定语 = in a century of art-market history（艺术市场史上持续百年的）
  谓语 = ended（结束）
  方式状语 = on a dramatic note（以戏剧性的方式；note = 格调/方式，熟词生义）
  伴随状语 = with a sale of 56 works by Damien Hirst（以一场赫斯特作品的拍卖）
同位语：Beautiful Inside My Head Forever → 解释前面 a sale 的名称
地点/时间状语：at Sotheby's in London on September 15th, 2008
💡 句式亮点：本句虽长但本质是简单句（一个谓语 ended）。理解长简单句的关键是：先找到谓语，再逐层剥离周围的修饰成分。
2
All but two pieces sold, fetching more than £70m, a record for a sale by a single artist.
除两件外全部售出，成交额超过 7000 万英镑，创下了单名艺术家的拍卖纪录。
🔍 语法解析
① 主干：All but two pieces（主语）+ sold（谓语）。All but two = 除了两件以外的全部。but = except（除了）。
② 结果状语：fetching more than £70m → 现在分词短语作结果状语，说明 sold 的结果。
③ 同位语：a record for a sale by a single artist → 补充说明 £70m 的意义。
💡 词义：fetch = 售得（熟词生义，不是"去拿"），考研常见。
3
It was a last victory.
这是最后的胜利。
🔍 语法解析
主干：It + was + a last victory（主系表）。短句但承载强烈暗示——"此后不再有胜利"（下一句雷曼破产）。考研阅读中短句常是段落转折的关键信号。
4
As the auctioneer called out bids, in New York one of the oldest banks on Wall Street, Lehman Brothers, filed for bankruptcy.
就在拍卖师喊出报价的同时，在纽约，华尔街历史最悠久的银行之一——雷曼兄弟——申请了破产。
🔍 长难句拆解
主句结构：
  主语 = one of the oldest banks on Wall Street（华尔街最古老的银行之一）
    同位语 = Lehman Brothers（具体说明是哪家银行）
  谓语 + 宾语 = filed for bankruptcy（申请破产）
从句：As the auctioneer called out bids → 时间状语从句（as = 正当…时）
💡 句式亮点：两个事件同时发生（赫斯特拍卖 vs 雷曼破产），暗示牛市终结与金融危机的因果关系。
Paragraph 2 — 艺术市场早已失去增长动力
5
The world art market had already been losing momentum for a while after rising bewilderingly since 2003.
全球艺术市场自 2003 年起令人眩目地飙升了一阵后，早已开始失去增长动力。
🔍 语法解析
① 时态：had been losing → 过去完成进行时，表示"在过去的某个时间点前一直在失去动力"。
② 时间顺序：after rising since 2003 → for a while → had been losing。
③ 词义：momentum = 动力；bewilderingly = 令人眩目地。
6
At its peak in 2007 it was worth some $65 billion, reckons Clare McAndrew, founder of Arts Economics, a research firm—double the figure five years earlier.
研究公司 Arts Economics 创始人 Clare McAndrew 估计，2007 年高峰期艺术市场价值约 650 亿美元——是五年前的两倍。
🔍 长难句拆解
主句：it was worth some $65 billion（主+系+表）
插入语：reckons Clare McAndrew → 引述结构，相当于 Clare McAndrew reckons that...
双重同位语：① founder of Arts Economics ② a research firm
破折号后：double the figure five years earlier → 补充说明 $65 billion
💡：考研常见"引述插入结构"，阅读时先跳过插入语找主干。
7
Since then it may have come down to $50 billion.
自那以后，它可能已降至 500 亿美元。
🔍 语法解析
① 情态动词+完成时：may have come down 表推测（可能已下降）。
② Since then：承接上文的 2007 年高峰。
8
But the market generates interest far beyond its size because it brings together great wealth, enormous egos, greed, passion and controversy in a way matched by few other industries.
但该市场引发的关注远超规模，因为它将巨额财富、自负、贪婪、激情和争议集于一身，少有其他行业能匹敌。
🔍 长难句拆解
主句：the market generates interest（主+谓+宾）
原因状语从句：because it brings together great wealth, enormous egos, greed, passion and controversy
方式状语：in a way matched by few other industries → matched by... 是过去分词短语作后置定语修饰 way
💡 词义：interest ≠ 兴趣，此处 = 关注/关注度。
Paragraph 3 — 赫斯特拍卖后，艺术市场急剧萎缩
9
In the weeks and months that followed Mr. Hirst's sale, spending of any sort became deeply unfashionable.
在赫斯特拍卖之后的数周和数月里，任何形式的支出都变得非常不流行了。
🔍 语法解析
定语从句：that followed Mr. Hirst's sale → 修饰 the weeks and months。
主干：spending ... became deeply unfashionable（主+系+表）。unfashionable = 不流行的。
10
In the art world that meant collectors stayed away from galleries and salerooms.
在艺术界，这意味着收藏家们远离了画廊和拍卖厅。
🔍 语法解析
承接：that 指代上句"spending became unfashionable"。
宾语从句：（that）collectors stayed away from galleries and salerooms。
11
Sales of contemporary art fell by two-thirds, and in the most overheated sector, they were down by nearly 90% in the year to November 2008.
当代艺术品销售额下降三分之二，在最过热领域截至 2008 年 11 月的一年中降幅近 90%。
🔍 语法解析
并列句：and 连接两个分句，从"整体降 2/3"到"过热领域降 90%"，递进。
数据表达：fell by two-thirds = 下降了三分之二；down by nearly 90% = 降了近 90%。
12
Within weeks the world's two biggest auction houses, Sotheby's and Christie's, had to pay out nearly $200m in guarantees to clients who had placed works for sale with them.
数周内，两大拍卖行苏富比和佳士得不得不向委托客户支付近 2 亿美元保证金。
🔍 语法解析
主干：the two biggest auction houses + had to pay out nearly $200m in guarantees
同位语：Sotheby's and Christie's
定语从句：who had placed works for sale with them → 修饰 clients
词义：guarantee = 保证金（拍卖行承诺的最低成交价保障）。
Paragraph 4 — 当前衰退是自 1989 年以来最严重的
13
The current downturn in the art market is the worst since the Japanese stopped buying Impressionists at the end of 1989.
当前艺术市场低迷是自 1989 年底日本人停止购买印象派作品以来最严重的。
🔍 语法解析
主干：The current downturn ... is the worst + since ...（时间状语从句）
💡 背景：1980 年代末日本泡沫经济时期大量收购印象派名画，泡沫破裂后戛然而止。作者用此历史事件类比当前。
14
This time experts reckon that prices are about 40% down on their peak on average, though some have been far more fluctuant.
此次专家估计价格平均比峰值下跌约 40%，尽管有些波动要大得多。
🔍 语法解析
宾语从句：experts reckon that ...（reckon = 估计/认为）
让步状语从句：though some have been far more fluctuant。
词义：fluctuant = 波动的（fluctuate + -ant）；down on = 比…下降。
15
But Edward Dolman, Christie's chief executive, says: "I'm pretty confident we're at the bottom."
但佳士得 CEO Edward Dolman 说："我很有信心我们已经见底了。"
🔍 语法解析
同位语：Christie's chief executive → 解释身份。
直接引语：says: "..." → 增加可信度。
💡：坏消息连篇后用乐观声音收尾——"we're at the bottom"（见底了）。典型的"先抑后扬"。
Paragraph 5 — 仍缺好作品而非买家
16
What makes this slump different from the last, he says, is that there are still buyers in the market.
他说，此次衰退与上次不同之处在于，市场上仍有买家。
🔍 长难句拆解
主语从句：What makes this slump different from the last（what = the thing that）
系动词：is
表语从句：that there are still buyers in the market
💡 句法：主语从句+表语从句嵌套。相当于：The thing that makes this slump different is that...
17
Almost everyone who was interviewed for this special report said that the biggest problem at the moment is not a lack of demand but a lack of good work to sell.
几乎所有受访者都说，目前最大问题不是缺乏需求，而是缺乏好的作品可卖。
🔍 长难句拆解
主语：Almost everyone + 定语从句 who was interviewed for this special report
谓语：said that ...（宾语从句）
从句内：not a lack of demand but a lack of good work to sell
💡 句式：not ... but ...（不是…而是…）考研高频。强调后者（缺好作品），否定前者（缺需求）。
18
The three Ds—death, debt and divorce—still deliver works of art to the market.
三个 D——死亡、债务和离婚——仍然将艺术品输送到市场。
🔍 语法解析
同位语：破折号中的 death, debt and divorce 解释 three Ds。
修辞：三个 D 首字母都是 D，简洁好记。
词义：deliver ... to = 输送到。无论市场好坏，死亡/债务/离婚总会迫使人们出售艺术品。
19
But anyone who does not have to sell is keeping away, waiting for confidence to return.
但不必出售的人都在观望，等待信心恢复。
🔍 语法解析
定语从句：who does not have to sell → 修饰 anyone。
主干：anyone ... is keeping away（观望）
伴随状语：waiting for confidence to return → 现在分词作状语。
💡：说明供给减少不是因为没东西可卖，而是持有者不愿低价出售。

📝 题目答案速查 ▼ 展开

21. Damien Hirst's sale was referred to as "a last victory" because ______.
A. witnessed a succession of victories B. the auctioneer got the two pieces C. won over all masterpieces D. made just before the financial crisis ✓
参考答案：D

🔍 定位：P1 S03-S04 — "a last victory. ... Lehman Brothers filed for bankruptcy" → 拍卖成功后雷曼破产，暗示金融危机到来。

❌ A 文中无据 B two pieces 是没卖出去的 C 夸大其词 D ✅

22. "spending of any sort became deeply unfashionable" suggests ______.
A. collectors no longer actively involved ✓ B. people stopped every kind of spending C. art collection had lost its appeal D. works of art were not worth buying
参考答案：A

🔍 定位：P3 S09-S10 — "collectors stayed away from galleries and salerooms" 直接解释了 unfashionable。

❌ B stopped 程度过强 C 不是艺术收藏过时 D 文中未说

23. Which of the following is NOT true?
A. Sales of contemporary art fell dramatically B. Surpassed other industries in momentum ✓ C. Market went downward in various ways D. Some dealers were awaiting better chances
参考答案：B（NOT true）

🔍 A→P3 S11 ✅ C→全文多处 ✅ D→P5 S19 ✅

❌ B：原文说 interest（关注度）远超规模，不是 momentum（增长动力）。偷换概念是考研常见干扰手法。

24. The three Ds are ______.
A. auction houses' favorites B. contemporary trends C. factors promoting artwork circulation ✓ D. styles representing Impressionists
参考答案：C

🔍 定位：P5 S18 — "deliver works of art to the market"（输送到市场）→ 促进流通的因素。

25. The most appropriate title is ______.
A. Fluctuation of Art Prices B. Up-to-date Art Auctions C. Art Market in Decline ✓ D. Shifted Interest in Arts
参考答案：C

🔍 全文核心：bull run ended → downturn / slump → decline。A 价格波动是细节；B 只覆盖第一段；D 未提。
