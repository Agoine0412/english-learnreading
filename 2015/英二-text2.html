第一代大学生的困境与出路First-Generation Students' Paradox

一项一小时低成本干预就能缩小 63% 成就差距的研究——打破阶层再生产的悖论
2015 考研英语（二） Text 2 教育社会学 难度 ★★★☆☆
💡 点击各板块标题旁「收起」可折叠内容 · 生词已用 高亮 标注 · 长难句带 ⭐ 标记
S01For years, studies have found that first-generation college students—those who do not have a parent with a college degree—lag other students on a range of education achievement factors.
📖 译文
多年来的研究发现，第一代大学生——即父母均无大学学位的学生——在多项教育成就指标上落后于其他学生。
主句结构studies have found that ... lag ...
that 从句found 的宾语从句，陈述研究发现的核心结论
破折号部分同位语，解释 first-generation college students 的定义——those who do not have a parent with a college degree
lag v.落后、滞后（及物动词，此处 lag other students = 落后于其他同学）
S02Their grades are lower and their dropout rates are higher.
📖 译文
他们的成绩更差，辍学率也更高。
grades n.成绩、分数（grade point average 的简称）
dropout rate n.辍学率（dropout = 辍学者）
平行结构A are ... and B are ... —— 两项并列对比
S03But since such students are most likely to advance economically if they succeed in higher education, colleges and universities have pushed for decades to recruit more of them.
📖 译文
但由于这些学生一旦在高等教育中取得成功，就最有可能实现经济跃升，因此几十年来各高校一直在努力招收更多这类学生。
since 引导原因状语从句说明高校积极招生的动机——经济回报驱动
advance economically在经济层面向上发展/提升社会经济地位
push for decades to do sth.几十年一直致力于/推动做某事；push = 积极推进/大力争取
S04This has created "a paradox" in that recruiting first-generation students, but then watching many of them fail, means that higher education has "continued to reproduce and widen, rather than close" an achievement gap based on social class, according to the depressing beginning of a paper forthcoming in the journal Psychological Science.
📖 译文
这就制造了一个"悖论"：大量招录第一代学生却又看着他们大批失败，意味着高等教育实际上"延续并扩大了而非缩小"基于社会阶层的成就差距——《心理科学》期刊即将发表的一篇论文开篇令人沮丧地指出。
📍 长难句拆解
This has created "a paradox"主句——指出当前局面构成悖论
in that = because引导原因状语从句，解释为什么说是"paradox"
recruiting ..., but then watching ...两个并列动名词短语作 in that 从句的主语成分，形成强烈对比
means that ...动名词短语的谓语，引出悖论的实质
continued to reproduce and widen, rather than close核心论点——延续并扩大而非缩小（reproduce = 再生产/复制原有模式）
based on social class过去分词作后置定语修饰 achievement gap——基于社会阶层的成就差距
forthcoming adj.即将发表的（论文/书籍等）
S05But the article is actually quite optimistic, as it outlines a potential solution to this problem, suggesting that an approach (which involves a one-hour, next-to-no-cost program) can close 63 percent of the achievement gap (measured by such factors as grades) between first-generation and other students.
📖 译文
但这篇文章实际上相当乐观，因为它勾勒出了解决这一问题的潜在方案：一种只需一小时且几乎零成本的干预方式可以缩小两类学生之间 63% 的成就差距（以成绩等因素衡量）。
📍 长难句拆解
as it outlines ...as 引导的原因状语从句，解释为什么说文章"乐观"
suggesting that ...现在分词作伴随状语，进一步展开方案细节
which involves a one-hour, next-to-no-cost program非限制性定语从句修饰 approach——强调方案的可行性（时间极短+成本极低）
next-to-no-cost复合形容词 = almost free / virtually no cost，"几乎零成本"
63 percent of the achievement gap量化效果——缩小 63% 差距，数据极具说服力
S06The authors of the paper are from different universities, and their findings are based on a study involving 147 students (who completed the project) at an unnamed private university.
📖 译文
该论文作者来自不同大学，其研究结果基于一项涉及 147 名完成项目的学生的研究，在一所未具名的私立大学进行。
involving 147 students现在分词短语作后置定语修饰 study
(who completed the project)非限制性定语从句补充说明 147 名学生的情况
unnamed adj.未透露名称的/匿名的——出于隐私保护不公开校名
S07First generation was defined as not having a parent with a four-year college degree.
📖 译文
第一代的定义是父母中没有一人拥有四年制大学学位。
was defined as ...被动语态，「被定义为……」
a four-year college degree四年制本科学位（美国高等教育的标准学制）
S08Most of the first-generation students (59.1 percent) were recipients of Pell Grants, a federal grant for undergraduates with financial need, while this was true only for 8.6 percent of the students with at least one parent with a four-year degree.
📖 译文
大多数第一代学生（59.1%）是佩尔助学金的受领者——这是一项面向有经济需求的本科生的联邦助学金——而这一比例在至少有一名父母拥有四年制学位的学生中仅占 8.6%。
📍 长难句拆解
Pell Grants佩尔助学金——美国最大的联邦大学生资助项目，以参议员 Claiborne Pell 命名，专供低收入家庭学生
recipients n.接受者/受领者（recipient 单数）
federal grant for undergraduates with financial needPell Grants 的同位语解释——面向经济困难本科生的联邦资助
while ... only for 8.6%while 引导的对比状语从句——59.1% vs 8.6%，悬殊的数据对比凸显经济差异
S09Their thesis—that a relatively modest intervention could have a big impact—was based on the view that first-generation students may be most lacking not in potential but in practical knowledge about how to deal with the issues that face most college students.
📖 译文
他们的核心论点——即相对温和的干预措施也能产生巨大影响——基于这样一种观点：第一代大学生最欠缺的可能不是潜力，而是应对大多数大学生所面临的实际问题的实用知识。
📍 长难句拆解
thesis n.论点/命题（学术论文的核心主张），注意不是"论文"本身而是其中的核心观点
破折号之间that 从句是 thesis 的同位语，具体阐述论点内容
modest intervention温和的/适度的干预措施（modest = 不大的/谦逊的）
based on the view that ...that 引导同位语从句解释 view 的内容
not A but B 结构not in potential but in practical knowledge —— 不是缺潜力而是缺实用知识，这是全文的关键判断
issues that face most college students定语从句修饰 issues——大多数学生都会面对的问题
S10They cite past research by several authors to show that this is the gap that must be narrowed to close the achievement gap.
📖 译文
他们引用多位学者的既往研究来证明：正是这种（实用知识的）差距必须被缩小才能最终弥合成就差距。
cite v.引用/援引（学术写作常用动词）
the gap that must be narrowed定语从句——指前文提到的 practical knowledge gap
close the achievement gap消除/弥合成就差距（close 在此处 = 缩小至零）
S11Many first-generation students "struggle to navigate the middle-class culture of higher education, learn the 'rules of the game,' and take advantage of college resources," they write.
📖 译文
他们写道，许多第一代大学生"难以适应高等教育的'中产阶层文化'、不懂'游戏规则'、也不会利用大学资源"。
📍 长难句拆解
struggle to do sth.费力做某事/挣扎着去做……（struggle = 挣扎、吃力）
navigate v.导航/穿越 → 此处比喻义：在……环境中找到方向、顺利前行
middle-class culture中产阶层文化——暗示美国高等教育体系本质上是为中产及以上家庭设计的
rules of the game游戏规则/潜规则——此处指大学运作的隐性规范（如何选课、找导师、申请资助等）
take advantage of充分利用/好好利用（固定搭配）
S12And this becomes more of a problem when colleges don't talk about the class advantages and disadvantages of different groups of students.
📖 译文
而当高校不去讨论不同学生群体的阶层优势和劣势时，这个问题就变得更加严重。
this becomes more of a problem when ...when 引导时间/条件状语从句
class advantages and disadvantages基于社会阶层的优势与劣势（class = 社会阶层）
don't talk about = avoid discussing / remain silent about回避讨论/对此保持沉默
S13"Because U.S. colleges and universities seldom acknowledge how social class can affect students' educational experience, many first-generation students lack insight about why they are struggling and do not understand how students 'like them' can improve."
📖 译文
「因为美国高校极少承认社会阶层会如何影响学生的受教育体验，许多第一代大学生不清楚自己为何苦苦挣扎，也不理解像他们这样的学生该如何改善。」
📍 长难句拆解
Because ..., many ... lack ... and do not understand ...因果复合句——因为（学校沉默）所以（学生困惑）
seldom acknowledge很少承认/几乎不承认（seldom = rarely）
how social class can affect ...宾语从句作 acknowledge 的宾语
lack insight about / into缺乏对……的洞察/认识（insight = 洞察力/深刻理解）
'like them'加引号表示特殊含义——指同样背景的第一代学生群体

📋 全文脉络▲ 收起

P1（S01-S04） 问题提出：第一代大学生成绩差、辍学率高；高校大力招录却反而扩大了阶层成就差距（paradox 悖论）
P2（S05） 方案提出：文章其实很乐观——一小时几乎零成本的干预能缩小 63% 成绩差距
P3（S06-S08） 研究方法：147 名学生参与；定义"第一代"；数据佐证——59.1% 领 Pell Grants vs 非 First-gen 仅 8.6%
P4（S09-S10） 核心论点：问题不在潜力而在于缺少实用知识（practical knowledge）；引用多份研究支持
P5（S11-S13） 深层归因：难以适应"中产阶层文化"、不懂"游戏规则"、高校回避谈论阶层议题导致学生迷茫无助

lagv. 落后于；滞后。常接 behind 或直接接宾语（lag other students）。n. lag 时间差/滞后。搭配：lag behind / fall into lag
achievement factorn. 成就因素/指标（复数 factors）。文中指衡量学生学业表现的多维度指标（成绩、毕业率、GPA 等）
dropout raten. 辍学率。dropout n. 辍学者；drop out of school 退学。反义：graduation rate / retention rate（毕业率/保留率）

reproducev. 再生产/复制/繁殖。本文指"再生产社会不平等"——阶层差异通过教育系统被复制传递给下一代（布迪厄理论核心概念）

outlinev. 概述/勾勒/列出纲要。outline a plan / outline a solution 提出计划大纲/勾勒解决方案
next-to-no-cost复合形容词 几乎零成本。类似表达：cost-free / virtually free / low-cost。next to = 几乎/差不多
private (university)adj. 私立的（大学）。public university 公立大学。美国高等教育双轨制

单词熟义 → 本文生义

✨ 值得背诵的句型▲ 收起

1. This has created "a paradox" in that ... 制造悖论句型（S04）
原文："This has created 'a paradox' in that recruiting first-generation students, but then watching many of them fail, means that ..."
模板：This has created "a paradox" in that [做法A], but then [相反结果B], means that [深层矛盾C].
用途：揭示表面合理的行为背后隐藏的自相矛盾或负面后果
2. ... continued to reproduce and widen, rather than close ... 再生产与扩大的递进（S04）
原文："higher education has 'continued to reproduce and widen, rather than close' an achievement gap"
模板：[主体] has continued to [动词1] and [动词2], rather than [反向动词3] [宾语].
用途：强调问题不仅未被解决反而恶化了，三个动词形成递进冲击力
3. may be most lacking not in A but in B 不是缺A而是缺B（S09）
原文："first-generation students may be most lacking not in potential but in practical knowledge"
模板：[对象] may be most lacking not in [表面缺失A] but in [真正缺失B].
用途：纠正大众误区——问题的本质往往不是人们以为的那个
4. Because ..., many ... lack insight about ... and do not understand ... 因果链式困境（S13）
原文："Because U.S. colleges seldom acknowledge how ..., many first-generation students lack insight about why they are struggling and do not understand how ..."
模板：Because [系统/机构] seldom [承认/面对][事实], many [弱势群体] lack insight about [自身困境] and do not understand how [出路].
用途：揭示结构性问题如何导致个体层面的双重困惑（不知因 + 不知果）
5. This becomes more of a problem when ... 问题加剧条件（S12）
原文："And this becomes more of a problem when colleges don't talk about ..."
模板：This becomes more of a problem when [条件/情境].
用途：引入使既有问题更加严重的附加因素，层层递进分析问题成因

26Recruiting more first-generation students has _____.
[A] reduced their dropout rates [B] narrowed the achievement gap [C] missed its original purpose ✓ [D] depressed college students
标准答案：C

🔍 定位：P1（S03-S04）。S03 说高校招录第一代学生的初衷是让他们"advance economically if they succeed in higher education"（通过高等教育实现经济跃升）。

S04 紧接着指出这制造了一个 paradox——招来了但大批失败，结果是"continued to reproduce and widen, rather than close" the achievement gap。

✅ C 正确推理：高校招录第一代学生的**原始目的**（original purpose）是通过高等教育帮助他们实现阶层跃升、缩小差距。但实际情况却是——招来了但大批失败，差距反而更大了——也就是说这个招录策略**偏离了/未能实现其初衷**。missed its original purpose 准确概括了这个悖论的本质。

❌ A 错误：reduced their dropout rates（降低了辍学率）——S02 明确说他们的 dropout rates are **higher**（更高），A 与原文方向完全相反。

❌ B 错误narrowed the achievement gap（缩小了成就差距）——S04 明确说"continued to reproduce and **widen**, rather than **close**" the achievement gap，B 恰好说反了。

❌ D 错误depressed college students（让大学生抑郁）——原文 depressing 形容的是论文的开篇令人沮丧（depressing beginning），不是说学生被弄抑郁了。D 属于偷换概念。

27The authors of the research article are optimistic because _____.
[A] the problem is solvable ✓ [B] their approach is costless [C] the recruiting rate has increased [D] their findings appeal to students
标准答案：A

🔍 定位：P2（S05）"But the article is actually quite optimistic, as it outlines a potential solution to this problem..."

✅ A 正确推理：文章之所以"quite optimistic"，正是因为它**找到了一个可行的解决方案**（outlines a potential solution）——一种只需一小时且几乎零成本的干预方式就能缩小 63% 的成就差距。这意味着问题是**可解决的**（solvable），这就是乐观的根本原因。

❌ B 错误：their approach is costless（方法是免费的）——原文说的是"next-to-no-cost"（几乎零成本），不是完全没有成本。而且即使免费也不是乐观的根本原因——乐观是因为问题**有解**。

❌ C 错误：the recruiting rate has increased（招生率提高了）——原文讨论的是招录之后学生失败的悖论，并未提到招生率变化。C 无中生有。

❌ D 错误：their findings appeal to students（发现对学生有吸引力）——文章是写给学术界和政策制定者的，appeal to students 不是作者乐观的理由。D 无关。

28The study suggests that most first-generation students _____.
[A] study at private universities [B] are from single-parent families [C] are in need of financial support ✓ [D] have failed their college
标准答案：C

🔍 定位：P3（S08）"Most of the first-generation students (59.1 percent) were recipients of Pell Grants, a federal grant for undergraduates with **financial need**..."

✅ C 正确推理：Pell Grants 是专门面向**有经济需求的本科生**的联邦助学金（with financial need）。59.1% 的第一代学生都领取了这项助学金——这说明绝大多数第一代大学生确实**需要经济援助**（are in need of financial support）。而对比组（至少一名父母有大学学位的学生）只有 8.6% 领取，数据对比非常鲜明。

❌ A 错误：study at private universities（在私立大学就读）——这项研究是在一所 unnamed private university 做的，但样本不代表所有第一代学生都在私立大学就读。A 以偏概全。

❌ B 错误：are from single-parent families（来自单亲家庭）——原文从未提及父母的婚姻状态（single-parent vs two-parent）。第一代的定义是"父母没有大学学位"，与是否单亲无关。B 无中生有。

❌ D 错误：have failed their college（大学失败了）——原文 S02 说 dropout rates are higher（辍学率更高）、S04 说 many of them fail（许多人失败），但 D 用现在完成时"have failed"暗示全部/普遍失败，过于绝对。而且 C 有明确数据支撑，D 没有。

29The authors of the paper believe that first-generation students _____.
[A] are actually indifferent to the achievement gap [B] can have a potential influence on other students [C] may lack opportunities to apply for research projects [D] are inexperienced in handling their issues at college ✓
标准答案：D

🔍 定位：P4（S09）"their thesis—that a relatively modest intervention could have a big impact—was based on the view that first-generation students may be most lacking not in potential but in **practical knowledge about how to deal with the issues** that face most college students."

以及 P5（S11）"Many first-generation students '**struggle to** navigate the middle-class culture...learn the 'rules of the game,' and take advantage of college resources."

✅ D 正确推理：inexperienced in handling their issues at college（在处理大学中的问题时缺乏经验）准确概括了两段的核心观点：

- S09：缺的不是潜力（potential）而是**实用知识**（practical knowledge about how to deal with issues）

- S11：struggle to navigate / learn rules / take advantage resources —— 三者都指向**缺乏处理大学生活的实践经验/能力**

"inexperienced" = 没经验的/不熟练的，正好对应 lacking practical knowledge 和 struggle to deal with。

❌ A 错误：are actually indifferent to（实际上不在乎/漠不关心）——全文都在关心第一代学生的困境并试图解决，A 与文章立场完全相反。

❌ B 错误：can have a potential influence on other students（可能对其他学生产生潜在影响）——原文说的是干预措施（intervention）对学生产生影响，不是学生对学生的影响。B 偷换主体。

❌ C 错误：may lack opportunities to apply for research projects（可能缺乏申请研究项目的机会）——原文从未提及 research projects（研究项目）。C 无中生有。

30We may infer from the last paragraph that _____.
[A] universities often reject the culture of the middle-class [B] students are usually to blame for their lack of resources [C] social class greatly helps enrich educational experiences [D] colleges are partly responsible for the problem in question ✓
标准答案：D

🔍 定位：P5 最后一段三句话的逻辑链条：

① S11：第一代学生 struggle to navigate middle-class culture（难以适应中产文化）

② S12：this becomes more of a problem when **colleges don't talk about** class advantages/disadvantages（当高校不讨论阶层议题时问题更严重）

③ S13：**Because U.S. colleges seldom acknowledge** how social class affects experience → students lack insight（因为学校很少承认阶层影响→学生缺乏认识）

✅ D 正确推理：colleges are partly responsible for the problem（高校对这一问题负有一定责任）——最后一段反复将矛头指向高校的不作为：

- 学校**不讨论**（don't talk about）不同群体的阶层优劣势

- 学校**很少承认**（seldom acknowledge）社会阶层对教育体验的影响

- 正是因为学校的这种回避/沉默，才导致学生"不知道自己为何挣扎、也不知道如何改善"。因此高校至少负有**部分责任**（partly responsible）。

❌ A 错误：universities often reject the culture of the middle-class（高校经常排斥中产阶级文化）——原文说的是第一代学生难以**适应**（navigate）中产文化，不是说高校排斥它。恰恰相反，高校本身就是中产文化的载体。A 方向反了。

❌ B 错误：students are usually to blame for their lack of resources（学生应为他们缺乏资源负责）——原文将责任指向学校的沉默和不作为（seldom acknowledge / don't talk about），而不是怪学生。B 与原文责任归属相反。

❌ C 错误：social class greatly helps enrich educational experiences（社会阶层极大地丰富了教育经历）——原文说社会阶层**影响**（affect）教育体验，而且是负面影响（造成不公平），不是"丰富"。C 把负面影响美化成正面效果。
