如何找到阅读的时间2016 考研英语（二）· Text 3

That everyone's too busy these days is a cliché — but there's never any time to read

难度 考研级词汇·语法·结构 主题 · 阅读与时间长难句 6
Paragraph 1 — 没人有时间阅读
1
That everyone's too busy these days is a cliché.
如今每个人都太忙了——这已经是老生常谈了。
🔍 语法解析
主语从句：That everyone's too busy these days 是整个句子的主语（that 从句作主语）。
词义：cliché 陈词滥调/老生常谈（法语借词）。
2
But one specific complaint is made especially mournfully: There's never any time to read.
但有一个具体的抱怨尤为沉痛：永远没有时间阅读。
🔍 语法解析
主干：one complaint is made（被动语态）。
冒号后：解释 complaint 的具体内容。
词义：mournfully 沉痛地/悲伤地（mourn 哀悼 + -ful + -ly）。
Paragraph 2 — 时间管理技巧为何无效
3
What makes the problem thornier is that the usual time-management techniques don't seem sufficient.
让问题更加棘手的是，通常的时间管理技巧似乎并不足够。
🔍 语法解析
主语从句：What makes the problem thornier = "让问题更棘手的事情"（what = the thing that）。
表语从句：that the usual time-management techniques don't seem sufficient。
词义：thorny 棘手的（thorn 刺 + -y）。
4
The web's full of articles offering tips on making time to read: "Give up TV" or "Carry a book with you at all times."
网上满是提供挤出时间阅读技巧的文章："放弃电视"或"随身携带一本书"。
🔍 语法解析
主干：The web's full of articles（主+系+表）。
修饰：offering tips on making time to read → 现在分词短语作后置定语修饰 articles。
5
But in my experience, using such methods to free up the odd 30 minutes doesn't work.
但根据我的经验，用这些方法腾出零散的 30 分钟并不奏效。
🔍 语法解析
动名词作主语：using such methods to free up the odd 30 minutes 作主语，doesn't work 作谓语。
词义：free up 腾出/空出；odd 零散的/临时的。
6
Sit down to read and the flywheel of work-related thoughts keeps spinning — or else you're so exhausted that a challenging book is the last thing you need.
坐下来阅读，与工作相关的思绪飞轮却不停旋转——或者你筋疲力尽，一本有挑战性的书是你最不需要的东西。
🔍 长难句拆解
并列结构：and 连接两个分句：Sit down to read（祈使句）= If you sit down to read。
后分句：the flywheel of work-related thoughts keeps spinning（暗喻——思绪像飞轮一样停不下来）。
or else：否则，引出另一种情况：you're so exhausted that ...（so...that 结果状语从句）。
词义：flywheel 飞轮；the last thing you need 最不需要的东西。
7
"The modern mind," Tim Parks, a novelist and critic, writes, "is overwhelmingly inclined toward communication... It is not simply that one is interrupted; it is that one is actually inclined to interruption."
"现代心智 overwhelmingly 倾向于交流……不仅仅是我们被打断，而是我们 actually 倾向于被打断，"小说家兼评论家 Tim Parks 写道。
🔍 长难句拆解
引语结构：Tim Parks 的引语被插入语（Tim Parks, a novelist and critic, writes）隔开。
引语内容：
  ① The modern mind is overwhelmingly inclined toward communication
  ② It is not simply that ...; it is that ...（不是……而是……的并列句型）
💡 核心观点：Parks 认为现代人不仅是被动地被干扰，而是主动倾向于被打断——这是深度阅读的敌人。
词义：overwhelmingly 压倒性地；inclined toward 倾向于。
8
Deep reading requires not just time, but a special kind of time which can't be obtained merely by becoming more efficient.
深度阅读需要的不仅仅是时间，而是一种特殊的时间——仅靠变得更高效是无法获得的。
🔍 语法解析
主干：Deep reading requires not just time, but a special kind of time（not...but 强调后者）。
定语从句：which can't be obtained merely by becoming more efficient → 修饰 time。
💡 Q31 考点：这句说明了时间管理技巧无效的原因——深度阅读需要"特殊的时间"，高效无法带来这种时间。
Paragraph 3 — "变得更高效"本身就是问题的一部分
9
In fact, "becoming more efficient" is part of the problem.
事实上，"变得更高效"本身就是问题的一部分。
🔍 语法解析
主干："becoming more efficient" is part of the problem。动名词短语作主语。
💡：这是全文最关键的观点转折——原本以为"高效"是解决方案，作者却说"高效"就是问题本身。
10
Thinking of time as a resource to be maximised means you approach it instrumentally, judging any given moment as well spent only in so far as it advances progress toward some goal.
把时间视为一种需要最大化的资源，意味着你以工具性的态度对待它，只有在某个时刻能推动某个目标的进展时，才认为它花得值。
🔍 长难句拆解
主干：Thinking of time as a resource to be maximised means you approach it instrumentally（动名词主语 + means + 宾语从句）。
伴随状语：judging any given moment as well spent only in so far as it advances progress → 现在分词短语，补充说明 instrumentally 的具体表现。
词义：instrumentally 工具性地；in so far as 只要/在……范围内。
11
Immersive reading, by contrast, depends on being willing to risk inefficiency, goallessness, even time-wasting.
相比之下，沉浸式阅读依赖于愿意承担低效、无目标甚至浪费时间。
🔍 语法解析
对比：by contrast 与上文的"工具性时间观"形成对比。沉浸式阅读与高效目标完全相反——它需要的是"低效"和"浪费时间"。
词义：immersive 沉浸式的；goallessness 无目标状态。
12
Try to slot it in as a to-do list item and you'll manage only goal-focused reading — useful, sometimes, but not the most fulfilling kind.
试着把它（阅读）塞进待办事项清单，你只能做到以目标为导向的阅读——有时有用，但不是最令人满足的那种。
🔍 语法解析
"祈使句 + and" 结构：Try to ... and you'll ... = 如果你……那么你会……。
破折号后：useful, sometimes, but not the most fulfilling kind → 补充说明这种阅读的局限性。
词义：slot in 塞进/安排进去；fulfilling 令人满足的。
13
"The future comes at us like empty bottles along an unstoppable and nearly infinite conveyor belt," writes Gary Eberle in his book Sacred Time, and "we feel a pressure to fill these different-sized bottles (days, hours, minutes) as they pass, for if they get by without being filled, we will have wasted them."
"未来像空瓶子一样沿着一条不可阻挡、近乎无限的传送带向我们涌来，"Gary Eberle 在他的《神圣时间》一书中写道，"我们感到一种压力，要在这些不同尺寸的瓶子（日、时、分）经过时把它们装满，因为如果它们没有被填满就过去了，那我们就浪费了它们。"
🔍 长难句拆解
引语结构：writes Gary Eberle 将引语分成前后两段。
第一段：The future comes at us like empty bottles ...（明确隐喻）
第二段：we feel a pressure to fill these different-sized bottles as they pass + for if they get by without being filled, we will have wasted them（for = 因为，引导原因状语从句）
💡 Q32 考点：这个隐喻说明人们感到有压力要把经过的时间填满（让时间过得充实）。
词义：unstoppable 不可阻挡的；conveyor belt 传送带。
14
No mind-set could be worse for losing yourself in a book.
对于沉浸于一本书来说，没有比这更糟糕的心态了。
🔍 语法解析
否定比较级表最高级："No ... could be worse" = 这是最糟糕的。"No mind-set could be worse" = 这种心态是最坏的。
词义：mind-set 心态/思维模式。
Paragraph 4 — 什么方法有效？
15
So what does work? Perhaps surprisingly, scheduling regular times for reading.
那么什么方法确实有效呢？也许令人惊讶的是：安排固定的阅读时间。
🔍 语法解析
设问：作者先提问再回答。does 表强调（确实）。
词义：schedule 安排（动词）。
16
You'd think this might fuel the efficiency mind-set, but in fact, Eberle notes, such ritualistic behaviour helps us "step outside time's flow" into "soul time".
你可能认为这可能会助长效率心态，但事实上，Eberle 指出，这种仪式化的行为帮助我们在时间流之外进入"灵魂时间"。
🔍 长难句拆解
转折并列句：but 连接两个分句。
  前分句：You'd think this might fuel the efficiency mind-set
  后分句：such ritualistic behaviour helps us "step outside time's flow" into "soul time"
插入语：Eberle notes 置于 but 之后。
💡 Q33 考点：ritualistic behaviour（固定时间阅读）帮助达到 immersive reading（沉浸式阅读）。
17
You could limit distractions by reading only physical books, or on single-purpose e-readers.
你可以通过只读纸质书或单一功能的电子阅读器来限制分心。
🔍 语法解析
主干：You could limit distractions（主+谓+宾），by reading ... 作方式状语。
18
"Carry a book with you at all times" can actually work, too — providing you dip in often enough, so that reading becomes the default state from which you temporarily surface to take care of business, before dropping back down.
"随时带一本书"实际上也确实有效——只要你足够频繁地沉浸其中，使阅读成为默认状态，你只是暂时浮出水面处理事务，然后再沉回去。
🔍 长难句拆解
主干："Carry a book" can actually work（主+谓）。
条件状语：providing you dip in often enough（providing = provided that = 只要）。
结果状语从句：so that reading becomes the default state ...
定语从句：from which you temporarily surface to take care of business → 修饰 default state。
现在分词：before dropping back down → 时间状语。
💡 Q34 考点：关键在于 "reading becomes the default state"（阅读成为默认状态）——阅读成为主业，其他事情只是临时打断。
19
On a really good day, it no longer feels as if you're "making time to read," but just reading, and making time for everything else.
在一个真正美好的日子里，你不再感觉像是在"找时间阅读"，而就是阅读，然后为其他事情安排时间。
🔍 语法解析
主干：it no longer feels as if you're "making time to read"（as if 引导表语从句）。
but 后：（you are）just reading, and (you are) making time for everything else → 并列省略。

📋 全文脉络 ▲ 收起

① P1: 提出问题——人人都说没时间阅读。
② P2: 时间管理技巧无效，因为深度阅读需要"特殊的时间"而非碎片时间；现代人倾向被打断。
③ P3: "高效"心态本身就是问题——工具性时间观 vs 沉浸式阅读的矛盾。
④ P4: 解决方案——固定时间安排（仪式化行为）创造"soul time"；让阅读成为默认状态。

📝 题目答案速查 ▼ 展开

31. The usual time-management techniques don't work because ______.
[A] what they can offer does not ease the modern mind [B] what challenging books demand is repetitive reading [C] what people often forget is carrying a book with them [D] what deep reading requires cannot be guaranteed
参考答案：D

✅ D（deep reading 所需的条件无法被保证）

定位：P2 S08 — "Deep reading requires not just time, but a special kind of time which can't be obtained merely by becoming more efficient."

推理：时间管理技巧只能让你更高效，但深度阅读需要"特殊的时间"（沉浸式不被打断的时间），这种时间是高效无法带来的。D = 深度阅读所需的条件（特殊时间）无法被保证（通过高效手段）。

❌ A：does not ease the modern mind（不能缓解现代心智）——S07 提到 modern mind 倾向于被打断，但这不是时间管理技巧无效的直接原因。B/C 文中未提。

32. The "empty bottles" metaphor illustrates that people feel a pressure to ______.
[A] update their to-do lists [B] make passing time fulfilling [C] carry their plans through [D] pursue carefree reading
参考答案：B

✅ B（让流逝的时间过得充实）

定位：P3 S13 — "we feel a pressure to fill these different-sized bottles (days, hours, minutes) as they pass"

推理：把时间比作瓶子，压力来自于"要把瓶子装满"——也就是要让每分每秒都过得充实（make passing time fulfilling）。

❌ A：to-do lists 只是 S12 的小点，与隐喻无关。C：carry plans through（贯彻计划）过度引申。D：carefree reading（无忧阅读）正好与此心态相反。

33. Eberle would agree that scheduling regular times for reading helps ______.
[A] encourage the efficiency mind-set [B] develop online reading habits [C] promote ritualistic reading [D] achieve immersive reading
参考答案：D

✅ D（实现沉浸式阅读）

定位：P4 S16 — "such ritualistic behaviour helps us 'step outside time's flow' into 'soul time'"

推理：soul time = 沉浸式阅读的时间。固定时间阅读的仪式化行为帮助跳出"时间流"进入 soul time → 实现沉浸式阅读。

❌ A：与原文相反——S16 说固定时间 not fuel efficiency mindset。B/C 文中未提或不是核心目标。

34. "Carry a book with you at all times" can work if ______.
[A] reading becomes your primary business of the day [B] all the daily business has been promptly dealt with [C] you are able to drop back to business after reading [D] time can be evenly split for reading and business
参考答案：A

✅ A（阅读成为你一天的主要事务）

定位：P4 S18 — "providing you dip in often enough, so that reading becomes the default state from which you temporarily surface to take care of business"

推理：default state = 默认状态 = 主要事务（primary business）。阅读成为常态，工作只是临时的打断。A 最准确。

❌ B：先处理完日常事务再读——不对，应该是阅读优先。C：读完能回到工作——这只是描述，不是条件。D：平均分配——不对，阅读应为主导。

35. The best title for this text could be ______.
[A] How to Enjoy Easy Reading [B] How to Find Time to Read [C] How to Set Reading Goals [D] How to Read Extensively
参考答案：B

✅ B（如何找到时间阅读）

定位：全文主旨。开篇"There's never any time to read" → 中间分析为什么没时间（效率心态） → 结尾给出解决方案（固定时间/仪式化行为）。全程围绕"如何找到时间阅读"。

❌ A：Easy Reading（轻松阅读）——文中强调的是 deep/immersive reading，不是 easy。C：Setting Goals（设定目标）——恰恰是作者批评的做法。D：Read Extensively（广泛阅读）——文中未提广泛性。
