2008 考研英语（二）· Text 4 精读

华盛顿与奴隶制——从"拔牙"案看开国元勋的历史复杂性
考研级 19 句逐解 上下单栏
📊 难度推断：考研级（依据词汇密度——peculiar / hamper / distaste / infancy 等考研词占比高）。注：这是根据词汇密度推断的难度，不代表文章来源限制。
S01
In 1784, five years before he became president of the United States, George Washington, 52, was nearly toothless.
🌐 译文
1784 年，即乔治·华盛顿成为美国总统的前五年，当时 52 岁的他几乎已经没有牙齿了。
语法分析
主干George Washington was nearly toothless（主系表）
修饰In 1784 时间状语；five years before he became president... 插入语补充时间；52 同位语补充年龄
语法同位语 · 时间状语从句
S02
So he hired a dentist to transplant nine teeth into his jaw—having extracted them from the mouths of his slaves.
🌐 译文
于是他雇了一名牙医，往自己下巴里移植九颗牙齿——而这些牙齿是从他的奴隶口中拔取的。
语法分析
主干he hired a dentist to transplant nine teeth into his jaw
修饰to transplant nine teeth 不定式作目的状语；—having extracted them... 现在分词完成式作伴随状语
语法不定式 · 分词完成式
🔥 拆解破折号后是核心考点：having extracted 是分词完成式，表示动作先于主句发生 → 先拔奴隶的牙，再移植到自己嘴里。
熟词生义 extract v. ① 提取/提炼（信息·汁液）；② 拔出（牙齿）；③ 索取/榨取（金钱）。搭配：extract teeth 拔牙 / extract information from。
S03
That's a far different image from the cherry-tree-chopping George most people remember from their history books.
🌐 译文
这与大多数人从历史教科书中记住的那个"砍倒樱桃树"的华盛顿形象大相径庭。
语法分析
主干That's a far different image from [the George]
修饰cherry-tree-chopping 复合形容词修饰 George；most people remember... 省略 that/whom 的定语从句，修饰 the George
语法定语从句省略 · 复合形容词
📚 文化"砍樱桃树的小华盛顿"典出美国教科书塑造的"诚实"传说（I cannot tell a lie），与本文揭露的"用奴隶牙补牙"形成强烈反差。
S04
But recently, many historians have begun to focus on the role slavery played in the lives of the founding generation.
🌐 译文
但近来，许多历史学家开始关注奴隶制在开国元勋一代人生活中所扮演的角色。
语法分析
主干many historians have begun to focus on the role
修饰[that/which] slavery played in the lives... 定语从句修饰 the role
语法定语从句
句型 the role [that] A played in B = A 在 B 中扮演的角色/所起的作用。写作万能句型。
S05
They have been spurred in part by DNA evidence made available in 1998, which almost certainly proved Thomas Jefferson had fathered at least one child with his slave Sally Hemings.
🌐 译文
他们的研究在一定程度上受到了 1998 年公布的 DNA 证据的推动——该证据几乎可以肯定地证明，托马斯·杰斐逊与他的女奴萨莉·海明斯至少育有一个孩子。
语法分析
主干They have been spurred in part by DNA evidence
修饰① made available in 1998 过去分词作后置定语，修饰 evidence　② , which proved... 非限制性定语从句，修饰整句　③ Jefferson had fathered... proved 的宾语从句（省 that）
语法分词作定语 · 非限制性定语从句 · 宾语从句省略 that
🔥 三层嵌套
spurred ← by DNA evidence (被什么推动?) └─ evidence ← made available in 1998 (何时公开的?) └─ which proved ... (证明什么?) └─ Jefferson had fathered a child with Sally Hemings
熟词生义 father 此处作动词——"成为……的父亲"即"与……生孩子"，不是名词！考研常考这种名转动。　spur v. 刺激、推动（be spurred by 被……推动）。
S06
And only over the past 30 years have scholars examined history from the bottom up.
🌐 译文
而且只是在过去 30 年里，学者们才开始自下而上地审视历史。
语法分析
主干scholars have examined history from the bottom up
语法部分倒装 ⚡ only + 时间状语 置于句首 → 主句部分倒装（have 提前到 scholars 前）。考研高频考点！
语法必考 only + 状语位于句首 → 主句部分倒装。例：Only then did I realize my mistake.　from the bottom up 自下而上地（从底层/普通人视角看历史，而非只看精英）。
S07
Works of several historians reveal the moral compromises made by the nation's early leaders and the fragile nature of the country's infancy.
🌐 译文
几位历史学家的著作揭示了美国早期领导人在道德上所做的妥协，以及这个国家初创时期的脆弱本质。
语法分析
主干Works reveal the moral compromises and the fragile nature
修饰made by the nation's early leaders 过去分词作后置定语修饰 compromises；of the country's infancy 介词短语修饰 nature
语法并列宾语 · 分词作定语
熟词生义 infancy n. ① 婴儿期；② （机构/组织的）初创期、初期阶段（本文义）。　fragile adj. 脆弱的。
S08
More significantly, they argue that many of the Founding Fathers knew slavery was wrong—and yet most did little to fight it.
🌐 译文
更重要的是，他们指出许多开国元勋知道奴隶制是错误的——然而大多数人却几乎没有为废除它而采取行动。
语法分析
主干they argue [that...]
修饰that many...knew slavery was wrong 宾语从句（内含省 that 的宾语从句）；and yet most did little... 并列的第二层意思
语法嵌套宾语从句 · 省略 that
短语 do little to do sth. 几乎不做某事（表消极态度）。
S09
More than anything, the historians say, the founders were hampered by the culture of their time.
🌐 译文
历史学家们说，最重要的是，这些开国元勋受限于他们那个时代的文化。
语法分析
主干the founders were hampered by the culture of their time
修饰More than anything, the historians say 插入语（评论性，可移到句首/句末）
重点词 hamper v. 妨碍、束缚、牵制（考研高频）。近义：hinder / impede / restrain。
S10
While Washington and Jefferson privately expressed distaste for slavery, they also understood that it was part of the political and economic bedrock of the country they helped to create.
🌐 译文
虽然华盛顿和杰斐逊在私下表达过对奴隶制的厌恶，但他们也明白，奴隶制是他们亲手创建的这个国家的政治和经济基石的一部分。
语法分析
主干Washington and Jefferson ... expressed distaste ..., they also understood [that...]
修饰① While ... expressed distaste 让步状语从句　② that it was part of... understood 的宾语从句　③ [that/which] they helped to create 定语从句修饰 the country
语法让步状语从句 · 宾语从句 · 定语从句
🔥 拆解核心逻辑是 While 引导的转折：前半句（让步）私下厌恶奴隶制 ✅ → 后半句（主句）但也明白它是国家政治经济基石 ⚠️ → 这种矛盾心理是全文核心论点。
熟词生义 bedrock n. ① 基岩（地质）；② 坚实基础、基石（抽象义，本文义）。　distaste n. 厌恶（比 dislike 正式）。
S11
For one thing, the South could not afford to part with its slaves.
🌐 译文
一方面，南方各州无法承担放弃其奴隶的代价。
语法分析
短语For one thing 一方面（常与 For another thing 连用）；part with 放弃、舍弃（含不舍之意）
熟词生义 part with phr. 放弃、舍弃（含不舍之意）。常接否定：can't afford to part with。
S12
Owning slaves was "like having a large bank account," says Wiencek, author of An Imperfect God: George Washington, His Slaves, and the Creation of America.
🌐 译文
《不完美的神：乔治·华盛顿、他的奴隶与美国之缔造》一书的作者温塞克说，拥有奴隶"就像拥有一个巨大的银行账户"。
语法分析
主干Owning slaves was "like having a large bank account," says Wiencek
语法动名词作主语 · 直接引语 · 插入语
修辞明喻（simile）——把拥有奴隶比作"巨额银行存款"，生动说明奴隶制的经济价值。
S13
The southern states would not have signed the Constitution without protections for the "peculiar institution," including a clause that counted a slave as three fifths of a man for purposes of congressional representation.
🌐 译文
如果没有对这一"特殊制度"的保护条款——包括将一名奴隶算作五分之一个人以计算国会代表名额的条款——南方各州就不会签署宪法。
语法分析
主干The southern states would not have signed the Constitution without protections for the "peculiar institution"
修饰including a clause 介词短语举例说明 protections；that counted a slave as three fifths of a man 定语从句修饰 clause；for purposes of congressional representation 目的状语
语法含蓄虚拟语气 · 定语从句
🔥 拆解
would not have signed (虚拟语气主干) └─ without protections (条件) └─ for the "peculiar institution" (保护对象 = 奴隶制的美化说法) └─ including a clause (具体保护条款) └─ that counted a slave as 3/5 of a man (五分之三条款) └─ for congressional representation (目的)
核心术语 the peculiar institution 是 19 世纪美国人对奴隶制的委婉称呼（euphemism），考试阅读中见到 ≈ slavery。　历史：五分之三妥协（Three-Fifths Compromise），1787 年宪法规定分配众议院席位时每 5 个奴隶按 3 个自由人算。
S14
And the statesmen's political lives depended on slavery.
🌐 译文
而且，这些政治家的政治生命依赖于奴隶制。
语法分析
功能段落主题句（Topic Sentence），统领整段论证。
S15
The three-fifths formula handed Jefferson his narrow victory in the presidential election of 1800 by inflating the votes of the southern states in the Electoral College.
🌐 译文
正是"五分之三公式"通过增加南方各州在选举人团中的票数，帮助杰斐逊在 1800 年总统大选中险胜。
语法分析
主干The three-fifths formula handed Jefferson his narrow victory
修饰in the presidential election of 1800 范围状语；by inflating the votes... 方式状语（by + doing）
熟词生义 hand v. ① 递给；② 交给、传递（结果/胜利）（本文义：hand sb. sth. = 使某人获得某物）。
S16
Once in office, Jefferson extended slavery with the Louisiana Purchase in 1803; the new land was carved into 13 states, including three slave states.
🌐 译文
杰斐逊一旦上任，就通过 1803 年的路易斯安那购地案扩展了奴隶制；这片新土地被划分为 13 个州，其中包括 3 个蓄奴州。
语法分析
主干Jefferson extended slavery; the new land was carved into 13 states
修饰Once in office 省略的时间状语（= Once [he was] in office）；with the Louisiana Purchase... 方式状语；including three slave states 补充说明
语法省略句 · 被动语态
📚 历史路易斯安那购地案（1803）：美国以 1500 万美元从法国购地，国土翻倍；后划为 13 州，其中 3 个蓄奴州，客观上扩大奴隶制地域。
短语 carve into 切割成、分割成（原意"雕刻"，引申为将领土划分）。
S17
Still, Jefferson freed Heming's children—though not Hemings herself or his approximately 150 other slaves.
🌐 译文
尽管如此，杰斐逊还是解放了海明斯的孩子们——尽管没有解放海明斯本人以及他大约其他 150 名奴隶。
语法分析
主干Jefferson freed Hemings's children
修饰Still 副词表转折；—though not... 让步成分省略，说明释放范围有限
S18
Washington, who had begun to believe that all men were created equal after observing the bravery of the black soldiers during the Revolutionary War, overcame the strong opposition of his relatives to grant his slaves their freedom in his will.
🌐 译文
华盛顿在独立战争期间观察到黑人士兵的英勇表现后，开始相信人人生而平等。他不顾亲属的强烈反对，在遗嘱中给予了奴隶们自由。
语法分析
主干Washington overcame the strong opposition ... to grant his slaves their freedom in his will
修饰① who had begun to believe... 非限制性定语从句修饰 Washington（超长插入）　② that all men were created equal believe 的宾语从句　③ after observing... 时间状语　④ of his relatives 修饰 opposition
语法非限制性定语从句 · 宾语从句 · 不定式作目的状语
🔥 骨架提取
Washington overcame opposition to grant freedom.
先剥修饰找最简主干，再逐层加回：who 从句解释改变原因；relatives 是反对者；in his will 是方式。
典故 all men are created equal 出自《独立宣言》（1776，杰斐逊起草）。极具讽刺：杰斐逊写了这句话却终身蓄奴，华盛顿却在看到黑人为国作战后真正信奉并释放奴隶。　grant v. 授予、给予（grant freedom）。
S19
Only a decade earlier, such an act would have required legislative approval in Virginia.
🌐 译文
仅在十年前，这样的行为还需要弗吉尼亚州的立法机关批准。
语法分析
主干such an act would have required legislative approval
语法虚拟语气 ⚡ would have required 表对过去的假设——十年前本需要立法批准，言下之意：法律变了，现在不用了。
重点词 legislative adj. 立法的（legislative approval 立法批准）。

全文脉络收起

引入：以华盛顿"用奴隶牙齿补牙"的惊人事实开篇，引发对开国元勋与奴隶制关系的重新思考（S01–S03）
新视角：DNA 证据 + 自下而上史学，揭示元勋道德妥协与时代局限（S04–S09）
经济依赖：南方经济离不开奴隶制（银行比喻、五分之三条款）（S10–S13）
政治依赖：政治生命与选举胜利依赖奴隶制（S14–S16）
迟来解放：杰斐逊释放海明斯孩子（不彻底）；华盛顿遗嘱解放所有奴隶——观念缓慢转变（S17–S19）
一句话概括：文章通过华盛顿拔牙案切入，揭示开国元勋虽在道德上认识到奴隶制错误，但因时代文化与政治经济的深层束缚大多未能有效反抗，仅少数人做出有限改正。

值得背诵的 3 个句型收起

句型 1 · While 让步 + 主句转折
While Washington and Jefferson privately expressed distaste for slavery, they also understood that it was part of the political and economic bedrock of the country they helped to create.
展现"虽然 A……但 B 也……"的矛盾心理，辩证思维满分。
写作模板：While X appears..., Y actually...
句型 2 · only + 时间状语 → 倒装
And only over the past 30 years have scholars examined history from the bottom up.
强调时间变化带来的认知转变，高分语法句型。
模板：Only in recent years have people begun to...
句型 3 · would have done without...（含蓄虚拟）
The southern states would not have signed the Constitution without protections for the "peculiar institution"...
表达"如果没有……就不会……"的假设条件，比 if 从句更简洁高级。
写作模板：... would not have happened without...

题目答案速查（36–40）收起

36. In the first paragraph, the author mentions Washington's dental practice to ___.
[A] show his unique way of living [B] praise his medical knowledge [C] emphasize his brutal treatment of slaves [D] reveal an unknown aspect of his life ✓
标准答案：D ✓

🔍 定位：P1 "George Washington, 52, was nearly toothless... hired a dentist to transplant nine teeth... from the mouths of his slaves."

✅ 正确答案推理：开篇用华盛顿"用奴隶的牙补自己牙"这个鲜为人知的事实，制造反差（对比教科书里"砍樱桃树"的诚实形象），目的是揭示建国者不为人知的一面（reveal an unknown aspect），引出全文主题。

❌ A 选项：文章不是说"独特生活方式"，而是借此引出严肃历史议题。

❌ B 选项：全文无任何"医术"评价。

❌ C 选项：文章语气是"历史复杂性"，并非单纯谴责"残忍对待"。

37. We may learn from the text that the Founding Fathers ___.
[A] could not tolerate contradictory ideas [B] were closely associated with slavery ✓ [C] fought against the political system [D] benefited from a strict legal system
标准答案：B ✓

🔍 定位：全文 P5–P7 "they were hampered by the culture..." / "political lives depended on slavery" / "three-fifths formula handed Jefferson his victory"。

✅ 正确答案推理：文章反复论证建国者与奴隶制深度绑定——道德上妥协、经济上依赖、政治上靠奴隶制获得选举胜利。B 的 "closely associated with slavery" 精准概括。

❌ A 选项：文中未提"无法容忍矛盾观点"。

❌ C 选项：建国者恰恰维护既有政治体系（宪法、选举人团），不是反抗。

❌ D 选项：法律是宽松/漏洞（五分之三条款），并非"严格法律体系"。

38. The author's attitude toward the Founding Fathers' view on slavery is ___.
[A] critical [B] supportive [C] complex / objective ✓ [D] indifferent
标准答案：C ✓

🔍 定位：P5 "knew slavery was wrong—and yet most did little to fight it"；P8 承认"迟来的解放"。

✅ 正确答案推理：作者既指出元勋知道奴隶制错误（批评），又理解他们受时代文化束缚（同情式解释），结尾还肯定华盛顿/杰斐逊的进步——态度是复杂而客观（complex / objective），并非单向批判或支持。

❌ A / B：都只是态度的一面，片面。

❌ D 选项：作者花了大量篇幅分析，绝非"漠不关心"。

39. Which of the following is true according to the text?
[A] Some Founding Fathers benefited politically from slavery ✓ [B] Washington freed all his slaves during his lifetime [C] Jefferson opposed the three-fifths formula [D] Slavery was abolished immediately after the Constitution
标准答案：A ✓

🔍 定位：P7 "The three-fifths formula handed Jefferson his narrow victory in the presidential election of 1800"。

❌ C 选项：杰斐逊是受益于该条款者，文中未说他反对。

❌ D 选项：宪法恰恰是保护奴隶制（五分之三条款），非立即废除。

40. Washington's decision to free his slaves originated from his ___.
[A] moral considerations [B] military experience ✓ [C] financial conditions [D] political stand
标准答案：B ✓

🔍 定位：P8 "Washington, who had begun to believe that all men were created equal after observing the bravery of the black soldiers during the Revolutionary War, overcame... to grant his slaves their freedom in his will."

✅ 正确答案推理：原文明确说华盛顿是在独立战争中观察到黑人士兵的英勇（military experience）之后，才改变观念、最终在遗嘱中解放奴隶。直接对应 B。

❌ A 选项：道德考量是其结果/表现，但原文给出的起因是战场见闻。

❌ C / D 选项：财务、政治立场文中均未作为"解放奴隶的起因"出现。
